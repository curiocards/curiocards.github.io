FAQ

1. How to get cards
  1. Create a Wallet at https://www.curiowallet.com
  2. Send ethereum to your Curio Wallet
  3. Send ethereum from your Curio Wallet to one of the Vending Machine Addresses
    1. Make sure you send enough to cover the price of the card
    2. If you send more, you can buy multiple cards at once
  4. Click “View your transaction”. If the vending machine was not empty, you will see a line that says ERC20 token. These are your cards!
  5. You can also see your cards listed when you access Curio Wallet now.

2. How to see your cards in MyEtherWallet.com

3. Are other wallets supported?
Sort of! As long as it's a wallet that you control the private keys for, you will recieve your Curio Cards just fine. However you won't be able to see them in most wallets with the exception of CurioWallet or adding custom tokens in to myetherwallet.com. So it's recommended you use CurioWallet.com or MEW! Devices like the Trezor and Ledger S nano are also compatable with CW and MEW, allowing you to easily store your Curio Cards on those hardware wallets.
We will be expanding wallet support over time.

4. What happens if I use an exchange wallet like Coinbase or Kraken?
Don't Do It
When you use a wallet on an Exchange you don't control the private keys. If you send ether to a Curio Card Vending Machine from an Exchange ethereum wallet, the exchange will get the cards and not you! Most exchanges will not go through the trouble of helping you track those cards down, and some can't even if they wanted to.

5. How can I get cards from previous weeks?
For cards that are our of stock, that either means the remaing supply was burned (like with cards curio 1-9), or the supply ran out. So they are no longer available from Curio Cards site or developers. The only way to get past cards are is to trade with current card holders who were clever enough to get some while they were still available. We have a marketplace telegram room to assist with this right now, and will be releasing new features to expand trading over time. You can find all that information on the Trading page.

6. I'm an artist, how can I make my own cards?
Welcome to Curio Cards, we are thrilled you found this project! One of Curio Cards main missions is to help artists get paid for there digital work, and we are going to be adding extensive features to support you over time. Currently the process of creating cards is rather manual, so we are featuring artists one at time over the course of a few weeks per artist. Please let us know who you are by submitting your information on the form located at the bottom of this page and Here. You can also join our Telegram rooms and/or Slack channel (links at the bottom of the page) to start learning more about the project and letting us know your ideas!
