FAQs

How to get cards
  1. Create a Wallet at https://www.myetherwallet.com
  2. Send ethereum to your MyEtherWallet (MEW) wallet
  3. Send ethereum from your MEW to one of the Vending Machine Addresses
    1. Make sure you send enough to cover the price of the card
    2. If you send more, you can buy multiple cards at once
  4. Click “View your transaction”. If the vending machine was not empty, you will see a line that says ERC20 token. These are your cards!

How to see your cards in MyEtherWallet.com
In MyEtherWallet click “Add Custom Token” for each of the current token

Are other wallets supported?
Sort of! As long as it's a wallet that you control the private keys for, you will recieve your Curio Cards just fine. However you won't be able to see them untill you load that private key into myetherwallet.com. So it's recommended you use MyEtherWallet (MEW)! Devices like the Trezor and Ledger S nano also are compatable with MEW, alloweing you to easily store your Curio Cards on those hardware wallets.
We will be expanding wallet support over time.

What happens if I use an exchange wallet like Coinbase or Kraken?
Don't Do It
When you use a wallet on an Exchange you don't control the private keys. If you send ether to a Curio Card Vending Machine from an Exchange ethereum wallet, the exchange will get the cards and not you! Most exchanges will not go through the trouble of helping you track those cards down, and some can't even if they wanted to.

How can I get cards from previous weeks?
For cards 1-9 at the end of the week (Weeks restart on Tuesdays) when it was dropped, we burn all unclaimed cards. This includes the full balance in the vending machines and the reserves. So they are no longer available from Curio Cards site or developers. The only way to get past cards are is to trade with current card holders who were clever enough to get some while they were still available. We have a marketplace telegram room to assist with this right now, and will be releasing new features to expand trading over time.

I'm an artist, how can I make my own cards?
Welcome to Curio Cards, we are thrilled you found this project! A Small and Wordy Glimpse into Curio Cards

Storing physical collectibles requires shelf space, plastic covers and cardboard boxes, while digital collectibles required trusting a company to keep your rare objects safe. Curio Cards changes all this with smart contracts. Store your own cards, and hold your own private keys. Our goal is to provide decentralized trading cards anyone can create, with trustless interactions and no compromises.
